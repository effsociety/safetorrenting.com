What is my IP address, and how can I be tracked with it?

Your IP address is a series of numbers used to uniquely identify your computer on a network. In other words, whenever you access a webpage on a remote server, your IP address is used to identify you to the server you are accessing. You can check your current IP address by clicking here. Take a moment to open that link. You may be surprised to see how much your IP address can actually reveal about you, even if it just looks like a string of digits. Most importantly, it leads straight back to your ISP (Internet Service Provider), who knows your real name, address, and other private information—and they can be forced by law to give this information up in certain circumstances.

When you download a torrent, your IP address is reported to the BitTorrent tracker; this is just the nature of the BitTorrent protocol. Anti-piracy groups monitor BitTorrent trackers and collect the IP addresses of users downloading copyrighted material. These IP addresses lead them straight to their ISP’s, who are required by law in most countries to give up their real names and home addresses. They’ll then have their lawyers mail you copyright infringement notices and fines.
