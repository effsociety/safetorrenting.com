Safe Torrenting

A guide to downloading torrents safely, and preserving your privacy and anonymity.

Governments are taking more efforts than ever to crack down on copyright infringement. In recent years torrent users from USA, UK, Germany, France, Australia and New Zealand have reported receiving fines of up to 2000 EUR just for downloading movies, music, books or software.

When you download a torrent, your IP address is reported to the BitTorrent tracker. Anti-piracy groups monitor these trackers and use your IP address to track you via your ISP, who are required by law to give up your real name and address. That’s how you end up with a copyright infringement notice and a huge fine—if you’re not careful.

In order to download torrents safely, you need to keep your IP address hidden. That way, whatever you download can never be traced back to you—thus ensuring your privacy and anonymity. The Safe Torrenting guide is a free resource that will walk you through various simple ways of accomplishing this.
